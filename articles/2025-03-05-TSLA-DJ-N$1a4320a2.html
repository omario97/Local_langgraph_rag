A thorny problem is growing at Tesla, where sales are notably slowing in some markets as vehicle shoppers steer clear of the electric vehicle company run by CEO Elon Musk, an increasingly polarizing figure because of his work as the Trump administration's chief federal government cost-cutter. 

   -- Morgan Stanley analyst Adam Jonas characterized recently weak sales 
      numbers as a "buyers' strike," as Musk's political activity turns off 
      core Tesla buyers -- those often politically left-leaning people looking 
      to go green by owning an EV. 
 
   -- Preliminary U.S. data do show a February rebound for Tesla sales. It sold 
      about 42,000 cars in the month, up about 14% from a year ago, according 
      to Ward's Automotive. But Tesla's February sales in France fell 26% from 
      a year ago. 
 
   -- Early numbers from the China Passenger Car Association showed Tesla's 
      February wholesale number, which includes exports and retail sales, 
      dropped 49%, likely affected by Tesla's Model Y changeover. The company 
      recently started selling an updated version of its best-selling vehicle 
      there. 
 
   -- The Chinese February drop follows January sales data that showed Tesla 
      underperforming in the U.S., Europe, and China. Sales fell in all three 
      regions from a year ago. Tesla shares are down 44% from their 
      postelection high of $488 a share in December.
