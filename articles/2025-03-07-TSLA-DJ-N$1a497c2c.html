Al Root

Tesla stock was falling in premarket trading after catching an unusual upgrade and a table-pounding call from a long-time bullish analyst.

At this point, any good news is welcome news for Tesla investors. After an incredible run, shares of the electric-vehicle maker have been mired in a brutal slump.

Tesla stock was down 0.6% in premarket trading Friday at $261.82, while S&P 500 and Dow Jones Industrial Average futures were rising 0.3% and 0.1%, respectively.

The upgrade should help shares. TD Cowen analyst Itay Michaeli late Thursday upgraded shares to Buy from Hold. TD Cowen's price target jumped to $388 a share from $180.

The target price jumped a whopping $208. Michaeli didn't exactly raise his target price that much. He came over from Citi where he rated Tesla shares Hold, but had a recent price target of $258.

Michaeli took over coverage at TD Cowen from Jeff Osborne.

However it happened, it's an upgrade and target price bump. "There are merits to both the bull and bear cases," wrote Michaeli. "While there is no shortage of challenges this year ... the list of potential game-changing level catalysts across, EV, autonomous vehicles, and robotics are robust enough to tilt risk/reward favorably."

The challenges are the potential removal of federal EV purchase tax credits worth up to $7,500, President Donals Trump's import tariffs, which raise Tesla's parts costs, and more EV competition. The positives are a new lower-priced EV due to be launched around mid-year, a self-driving robotaxi business expected by year-end, and a humanoid robotics business Tesla plans to ramp up in 2026.

Along with the upgrade, Wedbush analyst Dan Ives put Tesla stock on his firm's "best ideas" list as a "table pounder." A table pounder is a stock in which an has a strong conviction.

"This is a gut check moment for the Tesla bulls (including ourselves) after this massive selloff in Tesla shares with fears mounting," Ives wrote in a note.

Coming into Friday, Tesla stock has fallen about 46% since hitting an all-time intraday high of $488.54 on Dec. 18. Shares rocketed by some $237 after the Nov. 5 election. Initially, investors expected CEO Elon Musk's proximity to the president to benefit Tesla. Lately, investors have worried that Musk's political activities could be hurting sales.

"While the DOGE/Trump Musk iron clad partnership has created major brand worries for Tesla ... we estimate less than 5% of Tesla sales globally are at risk from these issues," added Wedbush's Ives. "We expect Musk will better balance his time between DOGE and Tesla/SpaceX over the course of 2025." Ives kept his Buy rating and $550 price target on the stock.

After Michaeli's upgrade, 49% of analysts covering the stock rate shares Buy, according to FactSet. The average Buy-rating ratio for stocks in the S&P 500 is about 55%. The average analyst price target for Tesla stock is about $379 a share.
